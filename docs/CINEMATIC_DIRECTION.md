# Cinematic Direction

Status: Draft production direction.

Primary reference: [CREATIVE_DIRECTION.md](./CREATIVE_DIRECTION.md)

## Core rule

Think like a film director.

Every shot must have one purpose:

- establish
- reveal
- build tension
- surprise
- transition

Avoid random camera movement.

## Approved shot language

Use:

- slow push-ins
- held wide shots
- low street-level frames
- shoulder-level tension
- foreground obstruction
- smoke reveals
- emergency-light silhouettes
- distant movement before direct threat

## Hero cinematic sequence concept

The Hero Production Candidate cinematic should feel like a single controlled scene:

1. Wide ruined city establishing frame.
2. Fog and ash reveal the checkpoint.
3. Emergency light flickers across abandoned vehicles.
4. A survivor presence is suggested, not fully explained.
5. Infected movement appears in the distance.
6. Logo emerges through smoke/firelight.
7. CTA appears after the world has established the emotion.

## Motion restraint

Not everything should animate at once.

Prioritize:

- drifting ash
- camera breathing
- fire flicker
- fog movement
- delayed logo reveal
- delayed headline animation

## Production warning

Do not use fast trailer chaos before the audience understands the world. The first hero should create dread and curiosity before spectacle.
