# Hero Production Candidate Brief

Status: Draft brief, internal review.

Primary reference: [CREATIVE_DIRECTION.md](./CREATIVE_DIRECTION.md)

The next hero milestone is **Hero Production Candidate**, not another technical prototype.

## Objective

Create one extraordinary hero that makes a new visitor think:

> I want to know what happened here.

The hero must feel like a paused scene from a major game cinematic, not a conventional landing page.

## Required scene content

The production candidate should include:

- ruined city
- abandoned vehicles
- emergency lighting
- drifting smoke
- burning debris
- infected movement in the distance
- survivor presence
- cinematic camera language
- official logo integrated into the world

## Composition priorities

- Foreground: ash, fog, smoke, UI, logo reveal.
- Midground: Survivor 001, Infected 001, ruined vehicle, abandoned checkpoint.
- Background: destroyed city, burning skyline, storm clouds, cinematic lighting.

## Production rule

One exceptional hero is preferred over many average sections.

Do not expand page scope until the hero has emotional impact.

## Technical foundation to preserve

The approved Hero Prototype V1 systems remain the foundation:

- cinematic layering
- motion sequencing
- sound opt-in flow
- controlled narration playback
- reduced-motion implementation
- WebGL fallback architecture
- registry lifecycle
- temporary asset handling
- CTA interaction pattern
- mobile-first layout philosophy

## Review requirements

The Hero Production Candidate review should include:

- production-quality cinematic
- first approved survivor concept
- first approved infected concept
- first approved environment concept
- final hero composition
- production audio direction
- real browser captures
- Lighthouse measurements
- Vercel preview available without access barriers for reviewers

Do not merge into main before review approval.
